=== GENERAL INFORMATION ABOUT NEMATODES ===

Nematodes (Nematoda) are microscopic roundworms found in soil, water, plants and animals.
Over 25,000 species of nematodes have been identified worldwide.
Plant-parasitic nematodes cause over $100 billion in agricultural losses annually.
They range in size from 0.1 mm to 8 mm in length.
Nematodes can survive in anaerobic conditions.
They are the most abundant multicellular animals on Earth.

=== PLANT-PARASITIC NEMATODES ===

Meloidogyne (Root-knot nematodes):
- One of the most economically important plant pathogens
- Form galls/knots on roots
- Attack over 2000 plant species
- Major species: M. incognita, M. javanica, M. arenaria, M. hapla
- Cause yield losses of 20-100% in susceptible crops

Globodera (Potato cyst nematodes):
- Quarantine pest in many countries
- Attack potato and tomato crops
- Cysts survive in soil for 20+ years
- G. rostochiensis (golden) and G. pallida (white)

Pratylenchus (Root lesion nematodes):
- Wide host range - attack most crops
- Enter root tissue causing lesions
- Create entry points for other pathogens
- Found in all agricultural soils worldwide

Heterodera (Cyst nematodes):
- H. avenae - cereal cyst nematode
- H. glycines - soybean cyst nematode
- H. schachtii - beet cyst nematode
- Females form protective cyst after death

Tylenchulus semipenetrans (Citrus nematode):
- Serious pest of citrus crops
- Semi-endoparasite
- Causes "slow decline" of citrus trees

=== NEMATODE LIFE CYCLE ===

1. Egg - laid in gelatinous matrix
2. J1 (first stage juvenile) - develops inside egg
3. J2 (second stage) - infective stage, migrates in soil
4. J3 (third stage) - inside plant
5. J4 (fourth stage) - near maturity
6. Adult - reproduces and lays eggs

Life cycle completes in 3-4 weeks at 25-30°C.
Up to 6-8 generations per year under favorable conditions.
J2 can survive months in soil without a host.

=== SYMPTOMS OF NEMATODE DAMAGE ===

Above-ground symptoms:
- Yellowing, wilting, stunting
- Nutrient deficiency symptoms
- Reduced yield
- Poor response to fertilization
- Patchy distribution in field

Below-ground symptoms:
- Root galls/knots (Meloidogyne)
- Root lesions (Pratylenchus)
- Cysts on roots (Heterodera, Globodera)
- Reduced root system
- Stubby roots

=== BIOPESTICIDE TECHNOLOGY ===

FUNGAL BIOCONTROL AGENTS:

Trichoderma harzianum:
- Most widely used biocontrol agent
- Destroys nematode eggs and juveniles
- Produces enzymes: chitinase, protease, glucanase
- Application rate: 1-5 kg/ha
- Compatible with most fertilizers

Purpureocillium lilacinum (formerly Paecilomyces lilacinus):
- Egg parasitoid of root-knot and cyst nematodes
- Effective against Meloidogyne and Heterodera
- Works best in warm, humid conditions
- Commercial products: BioAct, MeloCon

Pochonia chlamydosporia:
- Parasitizes eggs and females of cyst nematodes
- Persistent in soil
- Effective against Globodera and Meloidogyne

Arthrobotrys oligospora:
- Nematode-trapping fungus
- Forms hyphal networks to capture nematodes
- Adhesive networks and constricting rings

BACTERIAL BIOCONTROL AGENTS:

Bacillus thuringiensis:
- Some strains produce nematicidal proteins
- Cry proteins toxic to nematode juveniles
- Safe for humans and beneficial organisms

Bacillus subtilis:
- Multiple modes of action
- Produces lipopeptides (iturin, surfactin, fengycin)
- Enhances plant immunity (ISR)
- Improves soil microbiome

Pseudomonas fluorescens:
- Rhizosphere bacterium
- Produces HCN, siderophores, antibiotics
- Promotes plant growth (PGPR)
- Suppresses nematode populations

Pasteuria penetrans:
- Obligate parasite of Meloidogyne spp.
- Endospores attach to J2 juveniles
- Highly specific and effective
- Cannot be cultured in vitro - challenge for production

ENTOMOPATHOGENIC NEMATODES (EPNs):

Steinernema carpocapsae:
- Effective against surface-dwelling insects
- Works in ambush strategy
- Effective against caterpillars, weevils

Steinernema feltiae:
- Cold-tolerant species
- Effective against fungus gnats, thrips pupae
- Works at 10-25°C

Heterorhabditis bacteriophora:
- Cruiser strategy - actively searches hosts
- Effective in sandy/loam soils
- Works against grubs, weevil larvae

=== BIOPESTICIDE PRODUCTION TECHNOLOGY ===

TRICHODERMA PRODUCTION:

Substrate preparation:
- Rice bran or wheat bran as base
- Mineral salts: MgSO4 (0.1%), KH2PO4 (0.2%)
- pH adjustment to 5.5-6.5
- Sterilization: 121°C, 20 minutes (autoclaving)
- Moisture content: 60-65%

Solid-state fermentation (SSF):
- Temperature: 25-28°C
- Duration: 7-10 days
- Aeration required
- Target spore count: minimum 10^8 CFU/g

Liquid fermentation:
- Submerged fermentation in bioreactors
- Better control of conditions
- Higher scale production
- Duration: 3-5 days

Formulation types:
- WP (Wettable Powder) - talc or kaolin carrier
- WG (Water Dispersible Granules)
- SC (Suspension Concentrate)
- AS (Aqueous Suspension)

Quality control:
- Spore count (hemocytometer or plate count)
- Viability test (germination percentage >85%)
- Contamination check (plating on selective media)
- pH and moisture content
- Shelf-life testing

=== APPLICATION TECHNOLOGY ===

Soil incorporation:
- Apply 1-2 weeks before planting
- Rate: 2-5 kg/ha for fungal agents
- Requires adequate soil moisture (>60% WHC)
- Incorporate to 10-15 cm depth
- Avoid UV exposure

Seed treatment (bio-priming):
- Concentration: 10^7-10^8 spores/ml
- Soak seeds 30-60 minutes
- Dry before planting
- Improves germination and early establishment

Root dipping:
- For transplants and seedlings
- Concentration: 10^7-10^8 spores/ml
- Duration: 30 minutes
- Ensures direct root colonization

Drip irrigation:
- Apply through drip system
- Reaches root zone directly
- Efficient water and product use

=== INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) ===

Cultural practices:
- Crop rotation with non-host plants
- Deep tillage to disrupt nematode cycle
- Resistant/tolerant varieties
- Organic matter amendments
- Proper irrigation management

Biological control (primary strategy):
- Trichoderma + Bacillus combination
- EPNs for insect pests
- Antagonistic cover crops (Tagetes, Crotalaria)
- Soil solarization + biocontrol

Chemical control (last resort):
- Nematicides: Carbofuran, Oxamyl, Fluensulfone
- Fumigants: Metam sodium (restricted use)
- Rotate with biological methods

=== SOIL HEALTH AND MICROBIOME ===

Healthy soil microbiome:
- 10^8-10^9 bacteria per gram
- High biodiversity suppresses nematodes
- Organic matter supports beneficial organisms
- Optimal pH: 6.0-7.0

Suppressiveness:
- Naturally suppressive soils contain diverse microbial communities
- Repeated biocontrol application enhances suppressiveness
- Organic amendments increase suppression

=== LABORATORY METHODS ===

Nematode extraction methods:
1. Baermann funnel - for mobile nematodes
2. Mistifier/misting chamber
3. Centrifugal flotation - all forms
4. Cobb's sieving and decanting method

Identification:
- Morphological: light and electron microscopy
- Molecular: PCR with ITS-rDNA primers
- Perineal pattern for Meloidogyne species
- Body measurements (De Man's ratios)

Population threshold:
- Critical density for economic damage
- Potato: 50 Globodera eggs/g soil
- Vegetables: 200 Meloidogyne J2/100g soil
- Cereals: 1000 Heterodera/100g soil

=== ECONOMIC ANALYSIS ===

Damage levels:
- Light: 1-1000 nematodes/100g soil → 5-10% yield loss
- Moderate: 1000-5000 → 10-30% loss
- Severe: 5000+ → 30-70% loss

Cost comparison:
- Chemical nematicides: $200-500/ha
- Biological pesticides: $50-150/ha
- Crop rotation: minimal cost
- ROI of biocontrol: typically 3:1 to 5:1

=== FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ===

Q: Are biopesticides as effective as chemical nematicides?
A: Biopesticides work more slowly (2-4 weeks) but provide longer-lasting suppression. Under IPM programs, they can achieve 60-80% control comparable to chemicals.

Q: How long do Trichoderma survive in soil?
A: Under favorable conditions (moisture, organic matter, pH 6-7), Trichoderma can persist actively for 6-12 months.

Q: Can biopesticides be mixed with chemical fungicides?
A: Many fungicides are harmful to biocontrol agents. Always check compatibility. Copper-based fungicides and systemic fungicides are generally incompatible with Trichoderma.

Q: What is the shelf life of biological products?
A: Most products have 12-24 months shelf life when stored at 4-15°C, away from direct sunlight.

Q: How do I know if biopesticide treatment is working?
A: Root inspection after 4-6 weeks, soil population counts, and yield comparison are standard assessment methods.
